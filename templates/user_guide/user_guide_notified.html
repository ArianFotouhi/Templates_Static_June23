Data Absence

Stay ahead of data gaps with the alert sensitivity feature as well as alert mode.

Alert Sensitivity

To stay informed about data absence, the alert sensitivity feature proves to be an invaluable tool, enabling the detection of missing data from specific lounges or helping ascertain the activity status of clients. In essence, it serves as a user-defined threshold that determines the tolerable duration for not receiving new data. Once this threshold is surpassed, the respective inactive lounge is labeled as "dormant." This empowers users to proactively monitor data availability and promptly address potential gaps, ensuring accurate and up-to-date insights for effective decision-making.

Alert Mode

Within the client filters, the alert mode feature plays a vital role in organizing plots based on their alert status. Plots associated with clients or lounges that are considered dormant, due to data absence beyond the user-defined threshold, are displayed as the first priority. These dormant plots are clearly marked with a message indicating the data absence.

Following the dormant plots, the subsequent plots are arranged based on the highest rate of change percentage, whether it is an increase or decrease. The change percentage is calculated by considering the growth percentage between the last two data points of each plot.

By leveraging the alert mode ranking, users can quickly identify and address any dormant areas with missing data, allowing for timely intervention to rectify the situation. Simultaneously, they can focus on plots experiencing the most significant fluctuations, providing valuable insights into dynamic trends and patterns.

This comprehensive approach ensures that users are equipped with the most relevant and actionable information, enhancing their analytical capabilities and facilitating informed decision-making.
